# Cohort 1 — NGO Outreach Targets (15-30 candidates)

**Goal:** 5-15 design partners. Free pilot. Field feedback in exchange.

**Rule:** Never email `info@`. Find a real person. If you can't find one in
3 minutes — skip and move on.

---

## How to find the right person (3-min recipe)

For each NGO below:

1. **Open their website** → `/about`, `/team`, `/staff`, `/leadership`. Look
   for titles: *Security Manager, Safety Director, Head of Operations,
   Country Director, Programme Director, Director of Risk*.

2. **LinkedIn search** (the actual lever):
   ```
   site:linkedin.com/in "[Org Name]" "security"
   site:linkedin.com/in "[Org Name]" "safety"
   site:linkedin.com/in "[Org Name]" "duty of care"
   site:linkedin.com/in "[Org Name]" "head of operations"
   ```
   Google these literally. Top 3 results are usually right people.

3. **Email pattern**: most orgs use `firstname.lastname@org.org` or
   `firstname@org.org`. Once you have a confirmed pattern from one staff
   member's public bio/article — apply to others. Don't guess random
   addresses — bounces hurt your sender reputation.

4. **If stuck**: send via the org's general contact form ONLY when you've
   tried personal email and failed. Address it to "Attention: [Name],
   [Title]" — it usually gets forwarded.

## Email personalisation — what to put in [specific country/region]

The template line says: *"If [specific country/region they work in] makes
sense for this..."*

For each target, replace with the **single most active country** for that
org. Examples:
- White Helmets → "Idlib and NW Syria"
- East SOS → "Donetsk and Luhansk regions"
- Mona Relief → "Sana'a and Hodeidah"
- Hope for Haiti → "Les Cayes and the southern peninsula"

If you don't know which country off the top of your head — Wikipedia
"[Org name]" infobox usually lists "Areas served". 30 seconds.

## When you get a YES

Within 1 hour:
1. Reply with a single-line "Setting you up now — back in 15 min."
2. Create their account at `/account` (or send them the magic-link signup)
3. Pre-load their primary country/region as a watched location
4. Send them the link + a 2-line "what to look at first"

**Do not** do a sales call. They didn't ask for one. The whole pitch was
"no sales call".

When something flags in their region — **email them within 24 hours** with
a 5-line summary. This is the moment the relationship is built. Not in
month 3, not at a quarterly review. The first time you catch something.

---

## Honesty rules

- Don't oversell precision. Engine has 1 prediction in journal right now.
  Honest line: "We're early — engine improves with field feedback".
- Don't fake social proof. Don't say "trusted by 50 NGOs" until you have
  50 NGOs. Right now you have zero.
- Don't dodge "what does it cost". Free for them per criteria, paid for
  orgs above the threshold. Say it plainly.
- If an org replies "we already have Crisis24" — congratulate them, ask
  if there's a smaller partner org in their network this might help. Turn
  the rejection into a referral.
