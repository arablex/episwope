Why we do this

The orgs that need this most often can't afford it.

Crisis24, INSO and International SOS are excellent — and out of reach for most small NGOs. The teams running food distribution in eastern DRC, mine-clearance in northern Mali, medical missions across Sudan rarely have a procurement budget that compares.

We can't fix that whole market. What we can do is keep our engine open to the teams whose field reality teaches us more than any dataset — in exchange for honest feedback. That's the trade.

The deal

What you get. What we ask.

What you get
  • Real-time monitoring across 7 risk domains for every country you operate in
  • Country and event-level detail — pinpointed where the data allows
  • Email alerts to your security/safety focal point
  • Transparent scoring — every risk level shows why, with source links
  • No black box. No proprietary jargon. No expiry while you're in the pilot.
What we ask
  • Honest field feedback — what was useful, what was noise, what we missed
  • Permission to mention your org publicly (optional, your call)
  • One short call per quarter if you can spare it
  • That's it. Not a sales funnel.

Who qualifies for the free tier

  • Registered nonprofit (501(c)(3) or equivalent in your country)
  • Active field operations with staff in elevated-risk environments
  • Annual operating budget under $5M (or equivalent)
  • Not currently subscribed to Crisis24 / International SOS / INSO / Riskline / Control Risks

Not the free tier — but still welcome (paid)

  • Commercial organisations of any size — please see For Business
  • Universities and study-abroad programmes
  • Government-funded organisations above $5M annual budget

Free is for orgs we'd otherwise lose to the budget barrier. Anyone able to pay pays — or the price anchor disappears for everyone.
